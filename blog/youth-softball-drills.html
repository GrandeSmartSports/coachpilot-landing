5 Drills Every Youth Softball Coach Should Know

You don't need 50 drills. You need 5 good ones that work for any age group and any skill level. Here they are.

You don't need 50 drills. You need 5 good ones that work for any age group and any skill level. Here are the five I'd take to a desert island (if the desert island had a softball field).

1. Catch and Throw Warm Up

Partner up. Start close (15 feet), back up every 10 throws. Focus on: step toward partner, throw overhand, catch with two hands.

Why it works: This is the foundation of everything in softball. Every practice should start here.

2. Ground Ball Gauntlet

Line up single file. Coach rolls ground balls one at a time. Field it, throw to first, go to the back of the line.

Progression: Roll them harder, roll them to the side, add a baserunner.

3. Tee Work Station

Set up 2 to 3 tees. Small groups rotate through. Focus on: level swing, eyes on the ball, follow through.

For younger kids: Just making contact is a win. Celebrate it.

4. Relay Races (With a Twist)

Two lines, relay style. But instead of running, they throw to a partner, who throws to the next person. First team to get the ball down and back wins.

Why it works: Teaches throwing under pressure. Kids go absolutely nuts for this one.

5. Situational Scrimmage

Coach pitches (or use a tee). Set up a scenario: "Runner on second, one out. Where does the ball go?" Play it out. Then change the scenario.

Why it works: This teaches game sense better than any whiteboard session ever could.

Bonus tip: Rotate drills every practice. Same 5 drills, different order, small variations. Kids love routine with just enough surprise.
